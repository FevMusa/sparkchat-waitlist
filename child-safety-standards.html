Child Safety Standards

Effective date: June 16, 2026

Our Commitment

SparkChat, developed and published by Poacha Labs Pty Ltd, is committed to creating a safe environment for all users. We have an absolute zero-tolerance policy toward Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) in any form.

Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following is strictly prohibited on SparkChat:

  • Any content that sexually exploits or endangers children
  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) of any kind, including AI-generated material
  • Grooming behaviour or any attempt to solicit minors
  • Sharing, requesting, or distributing exploitative content involving minors
  • Any content that sexualises minors in any way
  • Using the platform to facilitate any form of child exploitation

Enforcement Actions

When we identify or receive reports of CSAE content or behaviour, we will:

  • Immediately remove the offending content
  • Permanently ban the offending user account without warning
  • Report the incident to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and/or relevant law enforcement authorities
  • Preserve relevant evidence as required by law
  • Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations

Detection and Prevention

SparkChat employs the following measures to prevent CSAE on our platform:

  • AI-powered content moderation to detect and block harmful content before it is delivered
  • User reporting mechanisms for flagging inappropriate content or behaviour
  • Regular review and improvement of our safety systems
  • Age-gating: SparkChat is rated 17+ and is not intended for use by children

Reporting Child Safety Concerns

If you encounter any content or behaviour on SparkChat that exploits or endangers children, please report it immediately:

Child Safety Contact

Email: hi@poachalabs.com

Subject line: CSAE Report — Urgent

We aim to respond to all child safety reports within 24 hours. In cases of immediate danger, please also contact your local law enforcement.

External Reporting

You may also report CSAE content directly to:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC): CyberTipline
  • Australian Federal Police: AFP Child Exploitation
  • Your local law enforcement agency

Policy Updates

This Child Safety Standards policy may be updated from time to time. Material changes will be reflected with an updated effective date at the top of this page.
